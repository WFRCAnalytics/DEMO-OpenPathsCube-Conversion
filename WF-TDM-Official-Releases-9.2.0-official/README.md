Before accessing the model, users **MUST** read the Release Letter below and adhere to its guidelines. Users must also have a GitHub account.

## Release Letter
This model and all supporting information are provided to your organization in good faith as a best effort level product. Use of any model version constitutes an acknowledgment of the following:

 - Appropriate application of the model and any interpretation of resulting model output to inform the formulation of a travel demand forecast or other decision support function is the responsibility of the model user.

 - Model users are encouraged to contact WFRC or MAG staff prior to any application of the model to request an in-person or virtual consultation meeting to discuss planned uses, suitable methodology, technical questions, and to ensure possession of the most current model release. *If this guideline is not followed, especially with new model releases, WFRC and MAG staff maintain the right to revoke access to the model and this GitHub repository.*

 - Model users may choose to make modifications to the model at their own discretion and risk, and are strongly encouraged to preserve a record of all changes and disclaim the presence and nature of all changes made when sharing or communicating any results.

 - Model users, at their earliest convenience, are strongly encouraged to provide feedback regarding model performance directly to WFRC or MAG staff. Similarly, requests for future model enhancements are encouraged.

 - Model users are requested not to redistribute this model but instead to refer any interested organizations or individuals to make a similar request for the model directly to WFRC or MAG staff. This will facilitate use of the most current model, opportunities for appropriate technical discussions, and additions to the Model User Group email list.

 - The model contains multiple networks that represent the road/highway and transit system in base, existing, and future years. The networks reflect funded Transportation Improvement Program (TIP) projects, both 'fiscally constrained' and 'need based' projects in the adopted Regional Transportation Plans (RTPs), and RTP amendments to-date. Additionally in the WFRC area of the model space, socioeconomic projections are included for both the 'fiscally constrained' and 'needs based' transportation investment scenarios.  

## Accessing the Model
Once users read and acknowledge the guidelines in the release letter, they may access the model. To access a specific version of the model, simply click the [Releases](https://github.com/WFRCAnalytics/WF-TDM-Official-Releases/releases) link on the right side of this page and then download the *Source code* under the *Assets* tab of the desired version. Alternatively, users may fork this repository and then clone it to their local machine.

## Submitting an Issue
If a user notices an issue with the model, it is recommended they submit a GitHub Issue [here](https://github.com/WFRCAnalytics/WF-TDM-Official-Releases/issues). Alternatively, users may contact WFRC or MAG staff to report the issue.
